5 Ways to Survive a Zombie Apocalypse You Probably Haven’t Thought Of

So, just because the Zombie Apocalypse hasn’t happened yet, doesn’t mean it won’t! Are you prepared if it does? What will you do!?!

Chances are since you’re on DIGA3008A-prep-site you’ve probably thought more about the zombie apocalypse than most.

There are tons of books and articles about basic zombie survival tips from fortifying your home to choosing the best weapon for killing walkers (it’s a machete). They mostly seem to have the same information. We took it a step further and compiled a list of ways to help you survive the zombie apocalypse.

Here are 5 advanced zombie survival tips you won’t find anywhere else.

1. Go to an Office Complex to Forage for Essentials

Offices are a great place to go to stock up on essential survival supplies. In an office park you can find 3 of the most important items to keep you alive: first aid kits, drinkable water, and non-perishable food. Few people think "business park" when they think survival, so there’s a good chance they haven’t been touched by other foragers. Plus, because no one goes to work during a zombie apocalypse there will probably be little to no zombies there.

Drinkable Water

One of the biggest obstacles to staying alive during a zombie apocalypse is dehydration. While dehydration affects seniors and young kids more often, even the most fit person can suffer from a lack of hydration. So finding potable water is key.

One of the best places to find clean drinking water can be in the office water cooler. Most offices have some kind of water dispenser. Many of these use 5-gallon water jugs — sealed and easy to transport — which can provide you with weeks’ worth of drinking water while you figure out a permanent solution.

While you’re foraging, look for a bottleless water cooler. These hydration machines usually have sealed tanks filled with drinking water, perfect for refilling canteens on the go. Plus, you can use the filters inside them for water filtration.

First Aid Kits

All businesses are required to have fully stocked first aid kits — it’s an OSHA safety requirement. You can find bandages, gauze, splints, and wound cleaning agents such as sealed moistened towelettes. Many kits also contain medicine like acetaminophen (Tylenol) and ibuprofen (Advil) to help reduce fever and inflammation, along with stomach medicine like Pepto Bismol.

Food

Vending machines are post-apocalyptic treasure chests. While many chips and candies won’t provide much nutrition, they offer high amounts of calories to keep you going until your next meal. A sweet candy bar can even offer a brief escape from the hellish apocalypse.

Because treats will become a rarity, vending machine items can also be used for bartering to get needed supplies. As a bonus, some vending machines even contain painkillers like Advil and Tylenol.

2. Sew Together Layers of Denim

One of the most immediate dangers of a zombie apocalypse is a bite from the rotting dead. Why not wear something to stop the bites? That’s where denim comes in. A single layer probably wouldn’t offer much protection, but in medieval times, poor soldiers wore layers of linen quilted together as armor — which worked decently well.

The same approach would work for zombies. Sewing together three layers of quilted denim could provide enough protection to stop a bite. Denim is also lightweight and breathable, so you won’t overheat while wearing it compared to riot armor or a leather jacket.

3. Get Some Spray Paint

Grab a couple cans of spray paint. Simply spray a tree or wall to mark where you’ve already explored. This helps your group find you if separated. You can also write messages to other survivors — to warn of danger or signal helicopters flying overhead. Just make sure to write legibly.

4. Stockpile Some Booze

While you don’t want to be drunk during a zombie apocalypse, it’s smart to collect high-proof alcohol. It works great for cleaning wounds and as an anesthetic. If you grab quality liquor, it also has trade value.

And yes — it can help numb the pain of loss and fear, but drink responsibly!

5. Drive an Electric Car

The biggest advantage of an electric car is how quiet it is — you can drive into a town without attracting zombies. Because they’re rechargeable, they won’t run out of fuel. You might think that’s useless without electricity in a zombie-ridden world, but you can solve that with solar power.

Set up your shelter with a solar installation to charge your car and other survival gadgets. Even a small setup of 10 solar panels can provide enough power to charge your vehicle’s battery.

Final Thoughts

Whether or not the zombie apocalypse ever comes, it’s always good to be prepared. By preparing ahead of time and getting your supplies in order — and keeping your mind sharp — you’ll have everything you need to survive. Just in case.
