Group Survival Kit

For small survivor groups staying alive — together.

In the apocalypse, teamwork can mean the difference between survival and extinction. The Group Survival Kit is designed for coordination, defense, and long-term sustainability. It contains everything a team of survivors needs to maintain health, morale, and mobility while defending against threats — both undead and human.

Shared Essentials

Water Filtration System: Filters large volumes for multiple people — more efficient than tablets.

Bulk Food Storage: Dried grains, canned protein, and long-life meals that can feed 3–5 survivors.

Extended Medical Supplies: Includes wound-closure tools, antibiotics, antiseptic spray, and trauma dressings.

Portable Cooking Gear: Compact stove, fuel tablets, and utensils for preparing safe meals anywhere.

Rechargeable Lighting Kit: Solar lanterns and headlamps to light up camps and perimeters.

Defense & Maintenance

Tool Set: Hammers, duct tape, rope, and nails for shelter repair and barricading.

Ranged & Melee Weapons: A mix of bows, bats, and firearms for flexible defense.

Armor & Safety Gear: Basic protective wear — gloves, goggles, and reinforced jackets.

Watch Schedule Board: Keeps everyone alert and ensures no one falls asleep on guard duty.

Navigation & Communication

Two-Way Radios: Essential for keeping your team connected across distances.

Paper Maps & Markers: Chart safe zones, supply caches, and known danger areas.

Signal Flares: For emergencies or to mark locations from afar.

Compass & GPS Device: Use both for redundancy — tech fails, but the basics don’t.

Weather Strategy

Zombies react differently under changing weather — plan as a team.

Rain: Ideal time for stealth travel — cover noise and movement with rainfall.

Heat: Rotate shifts and keep hydration constant; exhaustion kills faster than bites.

Cold: Build layered shelters, and maintain warmth using body heat and shared fire sources.

Team Tactics

Assign roles: Medic, scout, guard, cook, mechanic.

Rotate leadership: Prevent burnout.

Keep morale high: Talk, laugh, and remember you’re still human.

Store backups: Of every essential item.

Never split up: Unless absolutely necessary.
